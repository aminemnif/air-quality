Clustering Analysis of Air Quality Data

Overview

This project involves analyzing air quality data to identify patterns and insights using various clustering algorithms. The analysis aims to provide actionable insights for improving air quality and public health.

Data Used

The analysis was conducted using a dataset containing detailed air quality and health impact metrics.

  • Timeframe: 2002 to 2022
  • Pollutants: PM2.5, PM10, NO2, SO2, O3, CO
  • Metrics: Health Impact Score, Hospital Admissions, Cardiovascular Cases, Respiratory Cases

Methods

The project employed clustering techniques to identify patterns:

  • K-Means Clustering: Used to group data points based on similarity in air quality metrics.
  • Agglomerative Clustering: Provided hierarchical insights into relationships between different metrics.
  • DBSCAN Clustering: Identified clusters of varying density and outliers.

Findings

The analysis revealed the following key insights:

  • High pollution levels were associated with increased hospital admissions and health impact scores.
  • Clusters highlighted regional similarities in air quality and health impacts.
  • Outliers identified by DBSCAN indicated areas with extreme pollution levels.

Impact

The results of this analysis enable policymakers to prioritize regions and pollutants for targeted mitigation efforts, promoting cleaner air and better public health outcomes.
