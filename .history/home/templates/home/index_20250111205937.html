What is Air Quality?

Air quality refers to the condition of the air in our environment, which can directly impact human health. Poor air quality is caused by pollutants such as particulate matter, carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, and ozone, which can harm the respiratory system, increase the risk of heart disease, and affect overall well-being. Understanding air quality helps us take preventive measures to protect ourselves and improve environmental conditions.

Why is Air Quality Important?

Good air quality is essential for the health of humans, animals, and the environment. Prolonged exposure to air pollution can lead to chronic health conditions, including asthma, bronchitis, and cardiovascular disease. By monitoring and improving air quality, we can reduce these health risks and create a healthier, safer environment.
